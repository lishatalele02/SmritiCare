Understanding the Stages of Alzheimer's

Non-demented (Normal stage)

No memory or thinking problems. The person functions normally in daily life.

Very mild demented (Early stage)

Some small memory lapses (like forgetting names or recent events). These are mild and don’t interfere much with daily tasks.

Mild demented (Mild stage)

Memory problems become more noticeable. Trouble with planning, problem-solving, or remembering appointments. Daily tasks like cooking or managing money become harder.

Moderate demented (Moderate stage)

Memory loss is more severe. The person has trouble with more complex tasks, like driving or managing finances. They may also have trouble with language and getting lost in familiar places.

Severe demented (Late stage)

Memory loss is very severe. The person has trouble with basic daily tasks, like eating or dressing. They may also have trouble with communication and may not recognize familiar people.
